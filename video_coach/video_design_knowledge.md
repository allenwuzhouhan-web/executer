# Video Design Knowledge Base

## Pre-Production

### Script & Storyboard
- Define the video's purpose before anything else
- Break script into segments of 10-20 words or 20-30 seconds each
- Front-load the key message within the first 5 seconds
- Use conversational tone at 120-140 words per minute narration speed
- Plan shot variety: wide (establishing), medium (subject), close-up (detail)

### Cognitive Load
- Attention drops sharply after 6 minutes (NIH research)
- Break complex topics into 30-60 second segments
- One focal point per scene — viewers should know exactly where to look
- The 6-word rule: on-screen text should be 6 words or fewer per line
- Passive viewing = 20% retention; active learning elements boost retention by up to 20%

## Production

### Lighting
- Three-point setup: key light (main), fill light (soften shadows), backlight (separation)
- Natural light alternative: position subject facing a window
- Avoid mixed color temperatures (warm + cool = muddy look)

### Framing & Composition
- Rule of thirds: position subjects at grid intersections for balanced composition
- Keep backgrounds simple — clutter distracts from the subject
- Maintain 10% margin from all screen edges for text/content (safe zone)
- Keep critical content within center 80% of frame for cross-platform reformatting
- Leave bottom 20% clear for captions and platform UI elements
- Use no more than 3 layers per scene: background, main content, accent elements

### Camera & Motion
- Use tripod or gimbal — shaky footage looks amateur
- Add subtle zooms or pans to keep static shots interesting
- Change visual state every 3-6 seconds to maintain engagement
- Only one animated element at a time — avoid competing motion
- Use micro-pauses (2-3 seconds) between sections for information absorption
- Let key headlines sit on screen for at least 2 seconds for readability

### Audio
- Good audio is more important than good video — prioritize it
- Use external microphones: shotgun (directional) or wireless lavalier
- Ensure clear separation between narration and background music
- Use ducking: automatically lower music volume when narration plays

## Post-Production

### Typography
- Use one font family (Roboto, Open Sans, or Lato recommended)
- Main headings: 48-64px for 1080p video
- Body text: 28-36px for 1080p video
- Limit to two font weights: regular and bold
- WCAG AA minimum contrast ratio: 4.5:1
- Sans-serif fonts for screen readability
- Text animations: 200-400ms transitions, only when purposeful
- Leave 30% extra space for text expansion in localized versions

### Visual Hierarchy
- Contrast: make elements distinguishable, draw attention to key content
- Emphasis: highlight the most important element per scene
- Balance: no single element should overwhelm others
- Proportion: size signals importance — larger = more important

### Pacing & Rhythm
- Scene change every 20-30 seconds
- Strategic pauses (2-3 seconds) after key points for processing
- Keep training videos under 6 minutes or add chapter markers for longer content
- First 3-5 seconds determine if viewers stay — use a visual hook

### Editing
- Edit ruthlessly — remove anything that doesn't serve the message
- Use B-roll to illustrate points and add visual interest
- B-roll should illustrate concepts, not just decorate
- Use consistent transitions throughout — don't mix styles randomly
- Simple backgrounds for complex information; richer visuals for simpler messages

## Platform Optimization

### Aspect Ratios
- 16:9: YouTube, presentations, traditional video
- 1:1: social media feeds (Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn)
- 9:16: Stories, Reels, TikTok, vertical mobile

### Platform-Specific
- YouTube: optimize for search, strong thumbnails, first 5 seconds critical
- LinkedIn: 3-10 minutes, professional polish
- Social/mobile: assume sound-off initially, add captions
- Design mobile-first — most viewers watch on phones

## Branding & Consistency

### Visual Identity
- 75% of viewers recognize brands by logo, 60% by visual style, 45% by color
- Consistent branding boosts revenue by up to 23%
- Logo: always visible, subtle but present
- Color scheme: use a consistent palette across all videos
- One animation style per video — don't mix animation + live footage

### Style Guide Essentials
- Define: primary/secondary colors, font family + sizes, logo placement rules, safe zones
- Use consistent fonts across all videos (boosts brand recognition by up to 80%)
- Stick to one "look" across scenes — consistency wins over variety
- Repeat key visual elements and transitions for memorability
- Create reusable scene templates for team consistency

## Accessibility
- Always include captions with adequate contrast (WCAG AA)
- Body text minimum 24px
- Color contrast ratio at least 4.5:1
- Use color filters carefully — must match brand palette
- Darker filter backgrounds help lighter text stand out
